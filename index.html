Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam or Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was born on 15th October, 1931 in Rameshwaram, Tamil Nadu. He was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician and was the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He studied physics and aerospace engineering. He spent most of his career as a scientist and space administrator mostly at the Defence Research and Development Organisation(DRDO) or the Indian Space Research Organisation(ISRO). He has recievd several awards such as the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civillian award.

He is known as the Missisle Man of India as he was greatly involved in India's civilian space programme and military missile development efforts. He worked on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. He also played a very important role, both organisationaly and technicaly during India's Porkhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, the first after 1974.

He was elected as the 11th President of India in 2002 with supportt from the Bharati Janta Party (BJP), the ruling party, and the Indian National Congress(INC), the oppositon party. He was widely referred as the 'People's President'. He went back to his normal lfe of education and writing after just a single term.
